Travel Information

Entry Requirements – Tanzania

  • Visitors must have a valid passport with at least six months validity.
  • Most nationalities require a visa, obtainable on arrival or through online e-visa application.
  • Yellow fever vaccination is mandatory if arriving from an endemic country.
  • Tourist visas are typically valid for up to 90 days.

Climate – Tanzania

  • Coastal areas are hot and humid; the central plateau is dry and arid.
  • Cooler in the highlands (Arusha, Kilimanjaro) with chilly mornings and evenings.
  • Long rains: March–May; Short rains: November–December.
  • Best wildlife viewing: June–October (dry season).

Practicalities – Tanzania

  • Currency: Tanzanian Shilling (TZS); USD is widely accepted in tourism areas.
  • Health: Malaria risk exists; bring insect repellent and consult doctor for prophylactics.
  • Voltage: 230 volts, UK-type plug (three square pins).
  • Language: Swahili and English are widely spoken.
  • Communications: Mobile networks are strong; Wi-Fi available in most lodges and hotels.
  • Shopping: Look for Tinga Tinga paintings, wood carvings, Maasai jewelry, spices, and khangas.
  • Airports: Julius Nyerere International (Dar), Kilimanjaro International (JRO), and Zanzibar (ZNZ).

Entry Requirements – Uganda

  • A valid passport is required with at least 6 months validity.
  • Visas can be obtained online (e-visa) or on arrival for most nationalities.
  • Yellow fever vaccination is mandatory and proof must be shown at entry.
  • Multiple-entry East Africa Tourist Visa available for Kenya, Uganda, and Tanzania.

Climate – Uganda

  • Tropical climate, cooler in highland areas (Kampala, Fort Portal, Bwindi).
  • Rainy seasons: March–May and September–November; drier from December–February and June–August.
  • Best time for gorilla trekking: June–September and December–February.
  • Light clothing for the day and a warm layer for evenings in highlands recommended.

Practicalities – Uganda

  • Currency: Ugandan Shilling (UGX); USD accepted in tourist areas (notes must be recent).
  • Health: Malaria precautions essential; bottled water recommended.
  • Voltage: 240 volts, British-style three-pin plug.
  • Language: English is the official language; Swahili and Luganda also common.
  • Security: Use hotel safes and avoid isolated areas after dark.
  • Shopping: Hand-woven baskets, bark cloth, wood carvings, coffee, and vanilla beans.
  • Major Airports: Entebbe International Airport (EBB).
